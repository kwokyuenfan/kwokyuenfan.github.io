Lobbying and Fossil Fuel Investment

with Jianfu Shen, Yang Yu, Xin Yang, and Eddie C.M. Hui

*Business Strategy and the Environment* — Review and Resubmit

Using a sample of listed oil and gas companies in the U.S. from 2000 to 2019, we find that lobbying companies' capital expenditures increase by 6.2% and their oil and gas reserves' growth rate increases by 16.4% in the year following lobbying. Political lobbying by fossil fuel parent companies does not enhance abatement investment but instead raises pollution emissions.
